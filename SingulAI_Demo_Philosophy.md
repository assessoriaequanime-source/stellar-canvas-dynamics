# OBSIDIAN RITE

*A visual philosophy for SingulAI's threshold page — the door, not the room.*

---

## I. The Movement

**Obsidian Rite** rejects the visual grammar of contemporary AI products. No neon gradients, no rounded card stacks, no astronaut illustrations, no "Get Started" pills floating in purple haze. The landing of an artificial intelligence with persistent memory is not a *signup*; it is a *threshold*. This movement treats the entry screen as a frontispiece — the first page of a rare book, a museum vitrine label, the cover of a treatise privately printed in an edition of one hundred. The visitor does not click *into* a product; they cross *into* a rite.

## II. Form & Space

Composition is monumental, asymmetric, and quiet. A single dominant typographic gesture occupies most of the field — a serif italic phrase rendered at editorial scale, its letters allowed to breathe with the dignity of carved stone. Around it, vast obsidian negative space; against it, only the smallest possible counterweight: a hairline rule, a numeric coordinate, a date in archival format. The page must feel like it was *set*, not *designed* — as though a typographer at the top of their craft labored for weeks on the proportions of a single line. Margins are wide and deliberate, never decorative. Nothing centers itself out of habit; every placement is the result of a hundred refinements toward a quieter, more inevitable position.

## III. Color & Material

The palette is austere and total: deep obsidian black as the ground (not pure #000 — a black that holds a faint blue-violet shadow, the color of volcanic glass at midnight), bone-white for the primary type, and a single restrained accent — a cold cyan that appears only as a 1-pixel rule, a punctuation mark, or the faintest atmospheric haze in one corner of the field. No gradients across surfaces; gradient is reserved for *atmosphere only*, used like a photographer uses fog — to suggest depth, never to decorate. The black should feel materially present, as if the page were cut from stone or pressed from ink.

## IV. Scale & Rhythm

Disruption lives in the violent contrast of scale. One element commands the eye — a phrase, a single word, a roman numeral — at a size that borders on the architectural. Everything else recedes to whisper-scale: 8pt monospace coordinates, a tiny serial number, a colophon set in 6pt small caps. There is no middle register. This refusal of the comfortable mid-tier is the movement's signature: the eye is forced to choose between the monumental and the microscopic, and that forced choice creates the feeling of *consequence*. Every measurement should appear to be the product of meticulous calibration — not chosen, but discovered.

## V. Composition & Hierarchy

The page reads like an artifact, not an interface. A horizontal rule near the top carries a series of monospace coordinates: a date in archival ISO format, a sigil, a folio number, an institutional-style identifier. The center holds the rite — a serif italic statement, fragmentary, suspended. Below, near the lower margin, two thin lines of bone-white sans-serif offer the only choice the visitor can make, treated as inscriptions rather than buttons — set wide, lowercase, framed by hairline rules instead of pill shapes. A second horizontal rule at the bottom carries the colophon: domain, custodians, edition. The structure should evoke the title page of a book that has been waiting on a shelf for a hundred years.

## VI. Craftsmanship

This work must look as if it took a master typographer, a printmaker, and an archivist three weeks to agree on a single comma. Every alignment is intentional; every weight, the result of dozens of trials. The optical kerning of the display line must be hand-tuned, not algorithmic. The hairline rules must be exactly one device pixel — no thicker, no thinner. The microtype must remain perfectly legible at its smallest size. The whole composition should reward the visitor who lingers: a faint grid registration mark in the corner, a barely-visible watermark across the field, a sequence of dots that turns out to be a constellation. Nothing screams. Everything has been *labored over* with the patience of someone at the absolute summit of their discipline. The result is not a landing page. It is a *document*.

---

## VII. The Rite

> *"Inicie o rito de memória."*

Two inscriptions, set as the only interactive marks on the page:

— **com som**
— **em silêncio**

Both lead through the same threshold. Only the *manner* of crossing differs.
